Services

CyberVigilant Cybersecurity offers a range of cybersecurity services designed to help protect your business from cyber threats. Here are some of the key services that we offer

Network Security Assessments

Our network security assessments are designed to identify vulnerabilities and weaknesses in your network infrastructure, allowing us to develop a comprehensive security plan to mitigate these risks.

Penetration Testing

Penetration testing involves simulating a cyber attack on your network to identify vulnerabilities and weaknesses that could be exploited by hackers. Our team of experts will perform thorough testing to help you identify any potential security gaps and develop a plan to address them.

Security Information and Event Management (SIEM)

SIEM involves collecting and analyzing security data from various sources within your network to detect and respond to potential security threats in real-time. Our experts can help you set up and maintain a robust SIEM system to keep your business safe.

Cybersecurity Policy and Procedure Development

We can help your organization develop effective cybersecurity policies and procedures that are tailored to your specific needs and industry standards. These policies will outline best practices for your team to follow to help protect your business from cyber threats.

Frequently Asked Questions

At CyberVigilant Cybersecurity, we understand that our clients may have questions about cybersecurity and how it relates to their businesses. Here are some of the most frequently asked questions we receive, along with our answers.

  • What is cybersecurity, and why is it important?

    Cybersecurity refers to the practice of protecting electronic devices, networks, and data from unauthorized access or attacks. Cybersecurity is important because cyber threats are increasingly prevalent and can result in serious consequences, including financial losses, data breaches, and reputational damage.

  • How can I protect my business from cyber threats?

    There are several steps you can take to protect your business from cyber threats, including: Implementing strong passwords and multi-factor authentication for all devices and accounts Keeping software and systems up-to-date with the latest security patches Regularly backing up important data and storing it securely offsite Educating employees on safe cybersecurity practices and conducting regular cybersecurity training Conducting regular cybersecurity assessments and penetration testing to identify and address vulnerabilities in your network

  • What should I do if my business experiences a cyber attack or data breach?

    If your business experiences a cyber attack or data breach, it is important to take immediate action to minimize the damage. This may include: Isolating affected systems and devices to prevent the spread of the attack Notifying affected individuals and stakeholders of the breach Conducting a forensic investigation to determine the scope of the attack and identify the source Implementing remediation measures to prevent future attacks Contacting law enforcement or cybersecurity professionals for assistance

  • What are the most common types of cyber attacks?

    Some of the most common types of cyber attacks include phishing, malware, ransomware, denial-of-service (DoS) attacks, and man-in-the-middle attacks. Phishing attacks typically involve sending fraudulent emails or text messages to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information, such as passwords or credit card numbers. Malware refers to software designed to harm or exploit computer systems, while ransomware is a type of malware that encrypts a victim's files and demands payment in exchange for the decryption key. DoS attacks aim to overwhelm a network or server with traffic, while man-in-the-middle attacks involve intercepting and altering communications between two parties.
